Who Is Adonis Graham?

Her artistic work focuses on colorful, pop-art inspired portraits of cultural icons, musicians, and political figures. Drake's Journey to Fatherhood and Public Acknowledgment

Drake's path to publicly embracing fatherhood was complicated and messy. When Pusha T released "The Story of Adidon" in May 2018, the track exposed Drake's hidden son to the world. The diss track included lines about Drake hiding a child and questioned his readiness for fatherhood. This forced Drake's hand, leading him to address the situation on his album "Scorpion," released just weeks later in June 2018.

On "Scorpion," Drake dedicated multiple tracks to addressing his new reality as a father. The song "Emotionless" includes the lyrics: "I wasn't hidin' my kid from the world, I was hidin' the world from my kid." This line became Drake's explanation for keeping Adonis private during his first eight months of life. The track "March 14" (Adonis's due date, though he arrived in October) provides intimate details about Drake's feelings regarding unexpected fatherhood.

The rapper's initial hesitation stemmed from multiple factors. Drake has spoken about wanting to be certain of paternity before publicly claiming a child, given his celebrity status and past relationships. He also expressed concern about bringing a child into the intense scrutiny that accompanies his fame. These concerns, while understandable, conflicted with public expectations about parental responsibility and transparency.

By 2020, Drake's approach had completely shifted. He began posting photos with Adonis on Instagram, sharing their activities and bonding moments. The first public photo, posted in March 2020, showed Drake with Adonis and his parents, captioned with reflections on family and legacy.
